Blitz.gg Product Delivery & Content Engine – Case Study

Redefining go-to-market speed and scaling content pipelines across multiple games.

Role & Context

As a Product Manager at Blitz.gg, I oversaw the full delivery pipeline for game-integrated features across Valorant, League of Legends, and CS:GO. In parallel, I led the development of an internal content engine that scaled our production of educational and gameplay-enhancing media across millions of users.

Problem

With multiple feature teams working in parallel, our delivery timelines became bottlenecked by unclear ownership, cross-dependencies, and manual content workflows. Video and UI content were produced ad hoc, leading to delays and inconsistent quality across launches.

Solution

I introduced a structured delivery framework with standardized rituals, sprint planning, and QA phases across product, engineering, and content teams. Additionally, I designed and operationalized a scalable content engine an internal system for templated production, review, and publishing across multiple game titles.

Process

  • Rebuilt agile delivery cadence with bi-weekly sprint reviews, DRI ownership assignments, and integrated release QA gates.
  • Created a shared roadmap board with GitHub integration to align product, content, and engineering deliverables in real-time.
  • Designed and implemented an end-to-end content production workflow using Notion, Slack, and Google Drive to reduce manual ops.
  • Worked with leadership to forecast resourcing needs based on upcoming game releases and feature complexity.

Results

  • Increased sprint throughput by 28% across three cross-functional teams.
  • Reduced average feature delivery time by 36% quarter over quarter.
  • Scaled content creation to support 3x the volume with no increase in headcount.
  • Enabled simultaneous launches across Valorant, League, and CS with 95% on-time delivery.

Reflection

This initiative highlighted the importance of systems thinking in product operations. By treating content like code—with reusable patterns, QA, and iteration—we unlocked a new velocity tier for product delivery. Building trust between content and engineering teams proved essential, and this alignment became a cornerstone of future launches.
